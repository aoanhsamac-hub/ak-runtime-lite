## Overall Assessment

The Alkasik Kingdom constitutional framework demonstrates strong foundational alignment in core areas: Sovereignty (Art 1) is fully implemented across all charters and registries; the Risk Kernel (Art 5) is properly protected by the Risk Law with Sage as sole authority; Memory & Learning governance (Art 7, v1.1 Art 36-37) has comprehensive implementation via the Hermes Charter, Memory Law, and Knowledge Governance Decree. The executive governance chain functions with Janus coordinating all 7 agents through established escalation paths.

However, material gaps prevent full constitutional certification: the Constitution v1.1 FINAL source document remains inaccessible (CRITICAL), 5 of 7 constitutional agents lack FINAL charters (HIGH), the autonomous coding pipeline mandated by Art 8 is unimplemented (HIGH), and the emergency response automation demanded by Art 9 is not evidenced (HIGH). The State Corpus is a 23-line placeholder rather than a functional governance reference. The KTSP terminology migration (Kingdom rebranding, ~131 planned changes) has been fully planned but not yet executed.

Total assessed constitutional provisions: 15 (10 from v1.0 + 5 extractable from v1.1). Full alignment: 4 (27%). Partial alignment: 7 (47%). Missing/not evidenced: 4 (27%). No constitutional VIOLATIONS were found — the implemented system does not contradict the Constitution, but it does not fully realize all constitutional requirements.

## Recommendations for Kingdom Governance Freeze

The Kingdom Governance Freeze cannot be called until the following conditions are met:

1. **Critical blocker — Constitution v1.1 full text**: The authoritative v1.1 source must be extracted or re-commissioned. Without the full constitutional text, the freeze scope is undefined.

2. **High blocker — All 7 agent charters FINAL**: FINAL charters for Sage, Lang Lieu, Iris, Helen, and Yet Kieu must be drafted, reviewed by Sage, and approved by Hung Vuong, following the structure established by Janus and Hermes charters.

3. **High blocker — Autonomous coding pipeline**: The Art 8 pipeline (Issue→Plan→Code→Review→Test→Sage→Janus→Deploy) must be implemented as a governed workflow.

4. **High blocker — Emergency response playbook**: The 7 constitutional emergency triggers must have documented detection and response procedures.

5. **Medium blocker — State Corpus expansion**: The State Corpus must be expanded from 23 lines to a functional document enumerating agent-to-department-to-authority mappings.

6. **Medium blocker — Backward-looking compliance**: Existing implementations must be audited against backup-first (Art 3.1) and test-before-merge (Art 3.4) rules.

7. **Planned — KTSP execution**: The Kingdom Terminology Migration (6 waves, ~131 changes) should be executed before or during the freeze to avoid rework.

**Estimated freeze readiness**: After G1-G4 resolution, the framework can achieve CONDITIONALLY_CONSTITUTIONAL status. After G5-G7 resolution, FULL_CONSTITUTIONAL certification may be attainable subject to v1.1 verification.
