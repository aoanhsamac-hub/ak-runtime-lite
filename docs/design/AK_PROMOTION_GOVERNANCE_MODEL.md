## Purpose

This doctrine defines promotion gates for lessons, skills, and capabilities. Promotion is governance-controlled and never autonomous.

## Promotion States

Lesson:

```text
Draft
Reviewed
Approved
```

Skill:

```text
Draft
Reviewed
Approved
Active
```

Capability:

```text
Draft
Reviewed
Approved
Active
```

## Lesson Promotion Criteria

Draft to Reviewed:

- Complete evidence.
- Owner agent present.
- Source trace present.
- No secret exposure.

Reviewed to Approved:

- Hermes distillation complete.
- Sage risk review complete.
- Lesson quality score meets threshold.
- No unresolved contradiction.

## Skill Promotion Criteria

Draft to Reviewed:

- Evidence threshold met.
- At least 3 approved lessons.
- Pattern qualifies under skill discovery doctrine.

Reviewed to Approved:

- Hermes confirms evidence pattern.
- Domain reviewer confirms applicability.
- Sage confirms risk classification.

Approved to Active:

- Governance approval present.
- Rollback/retirement path exists.
- Skill changes recommendations only.

## Capability Promotion Criteria

Draft to Reviewed:

- At least 3 approved or active skills.
- Evidence shows broader decision improvement.

Reviewed to Approved:

- Hermes confirms skill cluster quality.
- Sage confirms risk.
- Domain reviewer confirms usefulness.

Approved to Active:

- Required approvers present.
- Constitutional/security/governance domains receive Hung Vuong approval if required.
- Capability remains recommendation-only.

## Risk Classification

Default:

- Low: isolated advisory learning.
- Moderate: cross-agent reuse.
- High: protected module recommendation.
- Critical: governance/security/execution-related learning.
- Constitutional: constitution, state corpus, risk kernel, or sovereign authority learning.

Highest Risk Wins.

## Rejection Workflow

Rejected records must record:

- Rejection reason.
- Reviewer.
- Evidence gap.
- Risk classification.
- Whether record is quarantined, revised, or retired.

Rejected records do not feed skill or capability discovery.

## Rollback Workflow

Rollback means removing a promoted record from active recommendation use.

Required steps:

- Mark record inactive or retired.
- Preserve audit trail.
- Record rollback reason.
- Notify owner agent, Hermes, Sage, and affected agents.
- Recompute dependent skill/capability confidence.

Rollback never deletes source records.

## Final Rule

Promotion controls knowledge visibility and recommendation use only. It does not grant new authority or modify production behavior.
